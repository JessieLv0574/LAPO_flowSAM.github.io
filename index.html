Adaptable World Models for Robotics

Advancing robotic perception and control through adaptable world models that can learn and generalize across diverse environments.

About the Project

Project Vision

Our goal is to develop world models that can adapt to changing environments and generalize across tasks, enabling more robust and efficient robotic systems.

Methodology

We combine deep learning, reinforcement learning, and probabilistic modeling to create adaptable representations of the world that can be used for planning and control.

Applications

Our research has applications in autonomous navigation, manipulation, and other robotic tasks that require understanding and interacting with complex environments.

Research Areas

World Model Learning

Developing models that can predict future states and outcomes based on current observations and actions.

Adaptation Mechanisms

Creating algorithms that allow models to quickly adapt to new environments and tasks with minimal additional data.

Generalization

Studying how world models can generalize across different domains, reducing the need for extensive retraining.
